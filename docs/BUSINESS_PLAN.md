# Autopilot-AI Business Plan

## Thesis

Autopilot-AI should sell a clear promise: turn Gmail and Calendar overload into a daily operating plan that a user can trust, control, and act on quickly.

The product is strongest when it is framed as:

- a personal operating system for high-input knowledge work
- source-backed AI, not a generic chat assistant
- privacy-aware planning, not blind inbox scraping

## Ideal Customer Profile

### Starter user

- solo founder
- student operator
- freelancer
- manager with a heavy inbox but limited budget

### Growth user

- startup operator
- chief of staff
- account manager
- recruiter
- founder with active customers, hiring, and investor communication

### Premium user

- executive
- chief of staff
- agency owner
- small team lead
- operator who wants controls, auditability, and higher-touch onboarding

## Problem

The target user is not missing a to-do list. They are missing a trustworthy system that can:

- read Gmail and Calendar context
- decide what actually needs action
- protect time on the calendar
- keep sensitive threads out of AI when needed
- show why a recommendation exists

## Product Positioning

Autopilot-AI is the cockpit for action-heavy work.

It is not:

- another generic task app
- another chat wrapper
- another inbox assistant that hides its reasoning

It is:

- Gmail-to-action extraction
- calendar-aware daily planning
- source-cited prioritization
- privacy-aware AI controls

## Offer Design

### Plan 1: Starter

- Price: `$5/month`
- Goal: make the product an easy yes for solo users
- Promise: "Get your Gmail into a usable daily action list."

Included:

- Gmail sync
- Google Calendar sync
- source-backed action extraction
- basic daily plan
- manual quick capture
- private sender AI blocker
- one connected workspace

Limits:

- limited sync volume
- limited saved planning runs
- no advanced admin controls
- no white-glove onboarding

Best for:

- students
- solo founders
- freelancers
- first-time users

### Plan 2: Pro

- Price: `$25/month`
- Goal: become the default plan for serious individual operators
- Promise: "Let AI help you run the day, not just list the inbox."

Included:

- everything in Starter
- AI daily planner with schedule blocks
- planning modes like Impact, Quick wins, and Deep work
- action lab workflows
- saved presets and workspace customization
- richer calendar planning
- more sync volume and planning runs
- priority support

Best for:

- chiefs of staff
- operators
- startup employees
- managers with constant inbound work

### Plan 3: Operator

- Price: `$200/month`
- Goal: justify premium spend with control, time savings, and white-glove setup
- Promise: "Run your work like you have an AI operator with guardrails."

Included:

- everything in Pro
- executive onboarding
- approval-aware automation workflows
- audit trail and enterprise-style controls
- organization policy support
- premium privacy controls
- higher usage limits
- fast support and setup help

Best for:

- executives
- agency owners
- team leads
- operators whose time is worth far more than the subscription

## Packaging Logic

The ladder should feel natural:

1. `$5` proves value with Gmail-to-action clarity.
2. `$25` captures the real daily-planning use case.
3. `$200` sells control, trust, and white-glove leverage.

This matters because the product should not try to force every user into the premium plan immediately. The pricing should create:

- a low-friction entry point
- a strong prosumer middle tier
- a high-margin premium tier

## Recommended Feature Gates

Starter should gate on scale, not on the core aha moment.

Starter gates:

- lower email sync limits
- fewer AI planning runs
- fewer saved action presets

Pro should unlock the full personal operator workflow.

Pro unlocks:

- full AI planning
- richer calendar blocking
- more saved presets
- more history

Operator should gate on control, service, and leverage.

Operator unlocks:

- policy controls
- onboarding help
- premium approvals and audit workflows
- concierge-style setup

## Go-To-Market

### Wedge

Lead with:

- founders
- chiefs of staff
- operators
- recruiters

Reason:

They already live in Gmail and Calendar, and the pain is immediate.

### Messaging

Primary message:

"Autopilot-AI turns Gmail and Calendar into a daily operating plan."

Secondary message:

"See what matters, block what is private, and protect time for the work that actually moves things."

### Acquisition

Early channels:

- founder and productivity communities
- X/Twitter build-in-public clips
- short Loom demos showing Gmail to plan in under a minute
- direct outreach to operators and chiefs of staff

### Demo flow

The best demo is simple:

1. Connect Gmail and Calendar
2. Sync
3. Show extracted actions
4. Block one private sender from AI
5. Run AI plan
6. Show the schedule blocks and source trail

## 90-Day Execution Plan

### Days 1-30

- make Gmail sync and planner output visibly trustworthy
- make the private sender blocker obvious
- finish Google verification materials and legal pages
- get stable onboarding for first live users

### Days 31-60

- push Pro plan positioning
- tighten source explainability
- collect time-saved testimonials
- instrument funnel conversion from sync to first AI plan

### Days 61-90

- pilot the `$200` offer with hands-on onboarding
- add admin and approval controls where needed
- close the first high-value users based on operational pain, not seat count

## Metrics

Track these first:

- visitor to sign-up conversion
- sign-up to source connection conversion
- source connection to first sync conversion
- first sync to first AI plan conversion
- weekly active planners
- number of blocked private senders configured
- paid conversion by tier
- retention at 30 and 90 days

## Risks

- Google restricted-scope verification can slow launch if the public app story is unclear.
- If source-backed planning feels fake or stale, trust collapses quickly.
- If premium pricing is pushed too early without enough operator leverage, churn will be high.

## Recommendation

Ship with this pricing logic:

- `$5/month` Starter as the entry offer
- `$25/month` Pro as the core plan
- `$200/month` Operator as the premium high-touch plan

The strategic goal is to make Pro the default paid plan, use Starter to reduce acquisition friction, and use Operator to fund high-trust onboarding and enterprise-style controls.
